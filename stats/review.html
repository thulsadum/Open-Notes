Probability Function:
  1. \mathbbP(A):Event→ [0,1]
  2. \mathbbP(∅) = 0, \mathbbP(Ω)=1, where ∅ is the empty set, and Ω is the problem space
  3. \mathbbP(A∩B) = \mathbbP(B)\mathbbP(A | B), Condition probability
  4. \mathbbP(A∪B) = \mathbbP(A) + \mathbbP(B) − \mathbbP(A∩B)
  5. \mathbbP(Ac) = 1−\mathbbP(A), Reason: A∩Ac = ∅∧\mathbbP(A∪Ac) = \mathbbP(Ω) ⇒ \mathbbP(A)+\mathbbP(Ac) = 1
  6. Given: A has mutually disjoint, \mathbbP(∪i=0n Ai) = ∑i=0n\mathbbP(Ai)
  7. \mathbbP(A)=∑i=0n\mathbbP(ti), assuming ti∩tj = ∅
  8. A ⊆ B → \mathbbP(A) ≤ \mathbbP(B)
  9. Independent iff: \mathbbP(A∩B) = \mathbbP(B)\mathbbP(B), alternatively written \mathbbP(A|B) = \mathbbP(A) for \mathbbP(B) ≠ 0
